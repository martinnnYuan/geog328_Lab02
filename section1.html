The topic I choose is Comfort & Health-Optimal Driving: Exposure-Aware, Road Situation-Sensitive Routing for Chinese Cities. The main purpose for this topic is to investigate how can we adjust the route selection by optimizing comfort and health on the road to improve the commuterâ€™s mental health during the day. Several aspects might be responsible for the comfort and health level during driving such as noise, greenness on the roadside, smoothness of the road surface, e-motor distribution, and air pollution. Studies consistently show that route choice can change exposure, sometimes without large time penalties. For example, choosing routes that minimize pollution rather than just travel time alters optimal paths (Alam et al., 2018), while commuter PM exposures varies by urban context and microenvironment, including in Chinese cities (Qiu et al., 2017; Kumar et al., 2021). Greenness on the roadside is associated with lower mortality and heart burden (James et al., 2016; Wu et al., 2024), and traffic noise is associated with cardiovascular risk through stress pathways (Munzel et al., 2024).
